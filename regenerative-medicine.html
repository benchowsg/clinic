Regenerative Medicine

Very often, after a debilitating illness such as a heart attack or stroke, there is massive damage to the body, resulting in severe loss of function.

Regenerative medicine is a growing, dynamic field that aims to restore, repair, and regenerate damaged or diseased tissues within the human body.

At its core, regenerative medicine focuses on harnessing the body's innate healing mechanisms to promote tissue repair, achieve functional restoration, or minimise damage.

Regenerative medicine offers new avenues for restoring function and improving quality of life post-illness, and it utilises revolutionary medical treatments from different disciplines across a wide range of conditions.

It holds tremendous promise for treating and improving various medical conditions across multiple fields that have shown a potential to improve the patient's cardiovascular health, respiratory, orthopedic, dermatological and autoimmune diseases.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is BNP blood test for?

    We have rapid blood tests to ascertain if you have heart failure. If your test exceeds certain levels, it may be indicative of acute heart failure.

  • What is Troponin-I blood test for?

    Certain heart enzymes may be leaked into the blood stream when there is heart muscle strain or damage. Our rapid blood tests together with ECG will be able to help you evaluate further if this is a true heart injury.

  • What is PT-INR blood test for?

    Some of us are on certain blood thinning medications to prevent blood clots from forming that might otherwise cause strokes. Such medications will require INR testing to monitor. We can do it for you and get results within the same visit.

  • What is electrolytes blood test for?

    Our body salt levels are important for normal body functions and any abnormal salt levels may lead to symptoms of giddiness, weakness, and palpitations.
