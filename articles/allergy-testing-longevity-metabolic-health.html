Is Your Immune System Working Against Your Heart?

"Dr. Thang, I eat 'clean' and I exercise, but I still feel puffy and sluggish. Why is my body resisting my efforts?"

In my clinic, I often find that the answer isn't a lack of discipline; it's a lack of Immune Stewardship.

In Singapore, we are conditioned to "tahan" (endure) minor discomforts. However, chronic low-grade inflammation is now recognized by the global medical community as a primary driver of non-communicable diseases[1]. From a longevity perspective, these are early warning signs of Immune Friction.

The Cardiovascular Connection: Vascular Sandpaper

When your body reacts to an allergen, it releases histamine and pro-inflammatory cytokines. While histamine helps fight invaders, in a chronic "low-dose" state, it acts like vascular sandpaper. It creates microscopic irritation in the endothelium (the inner lining of your arteries)[2].

This irritation reduces the bioavailability of Nitric Oxide—the molecule that keeps your arteries flexible. Research in the Journal of the American College of Cardiology suggests that such chronic inflammatory triggers significantly accelerate Arterial Stiffness, potentially worsening your Calcium Score over time[3].

The Metabolic Trap: Why 'Healthy' Eating Isn't Enough

If you are struggling with stubborn midsection weight, you may be stuck in the Metabolic-Immune Trap. When your body identifies a "healthy" food as an invader, it triggers a cortisol response. Chronic elevations in cortisol are clinically linked to Insulin Resistance and increased visceral adiposity (internal fat)[4].

In Singapore, where 1 in 4 residents suffer from Metabolic Syndrome, identifying these hidden triggers is essential. Data from the Lancet indicates that addressing systemic inflammation is as crucial as managing cholesterol for long-term survival[5].
