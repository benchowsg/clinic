Immune Friction: Why Your "Healthy" Choices Might Be Aging Your Heart

Most people view allergies as an occasional inconvenience. In longevity medicine, we identify them as "Immune Friction"—a silent driver of vascular decay and metabolic resistance.

The "Why": Beyond the Itch

An undiagnosed allergy is essentially a low-grade internal forest fire. It keeps your immune system in a state of constant aggression, which affects three major systems:

  • Vascular Endothelium: Inflammation irritates the inner lining of your arteries, reducing elasticity.
  • The Cortisol Spike: Allergic stress triggers cortisol, the primary driver of Insulin Resistance.
  • Autonomic Fatigue: Constant immune activity lowers your Heart Rate Variability (HRV).

The Systemic Friction Loop: How low-grade immune triggers bypass the "itch" and go straight to the heart and metabolism.

Who Benefits Most?

The Plateaued Achiever

Those who eat "clean" but struggle with persistent midsection fat. An audit often reveals "healthy" foods acting as triggers.

The Foggy Executive

High-performers dealing with post-meal brain fog. Clearing "immune noise" restores cognitive edge.

The Stiff Patient

Individuals over 40 noticing joint stiffness or rising blood pressure. Lowering allergic load preserves vascular elasticity.

The Stewardship Protocol

Knowing is 10% of the battle. Our clinical strategy focuses on Resiliency:

  1. Load Reduction: Removing the top 20% of triggers to lower systemic noise.
  2. Barrier Repair: Healing the gut and skin to prevent future sensitivities.
  3. Vascular Monitoring: Ensuring your heart's flexibility returns as inflammation drops.
