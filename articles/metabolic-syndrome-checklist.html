The Syndrome X Checklist

Metabolic Syndrome (Syndrome X) is not a single disease, but a cluster of five specific markers. Having three or more of these increases your risk of heart disease and stroke significantly.

1

Abdominal Obesity

Excess visceral fat around the midsection is a primary driver of systemic inflammation.

Threshold (Asian Context):
Men: > 90cm | Women: > 80cm
2

Elevated Blood Pressure

Consistent pressure against arterial walls, even at "borderline" levels.

Threshold:
$\ge$ 130/85 mmHg
3

High Triglycerides

The amount of fat circulating in your blood, often linked to sugar intake.

Threshold:
$\ge$ 1.7 mmol/L
4

Low HDL Cholesterol

HDL is the "clean-up crew" for your arteries. Too little is a risk factor.

Threshold:
Men: < 1.03 mmol/L | Women: < 1.29 mmol/L
5

High Fasting Glucose

An early indicator of insulin resistance and pre-diabetes.

Threshold:
$\ge$ 5.6 mmol/L

The "Rule of Three"

If you meet 3 or more of these criteria, you are clinically diagnosed with Metabolic Syndrome. This is a critical signal that your cardiovascular system is under metabolic stress.
