The 3 Risk Eras

Ages 20–39: The Prevention Era

At this stage, we focus on establishing a metabolic baseline. We rarely start statins unless there is a severe genetic predisposition, as your "pipes" are still young and resilient.

Ages 40–59: The "Red Zone" (Stabilization Era)

Metabolic buffering often fails here. A statin is used as "plaque glue" to harden soft plaque before it ruptures. Starting now can effectively reset your cardiovascular clock.

Ages 60+: The Preservation Era

The focus shifts to event prevention. Statins are used to reduce systemic inflammation and protect your long-term vascular elasticity from cumulative wear and tear.

Your Pre-Consult Checklist

Before your visit, please note down the following:

  • ✅ Muscle Baseline: Are you sore from training, or is this new?
  • ✅ Genetic History: Did any family members have events in their 40s or 50s?
  • ✅ Supplement Stack: Bring your Red Yeast Rice or Fish Oil bottles.

The Exit Strategy

Statins are not necessarily "forever." If you achieve a Metabolic Pivot (10–15% weight loss and significant dietary shift), we can re-audit your infrastructure to see if medication can be safely paused.
