2. Reversibility vs. Permanent Scarring

The greatest cost of waiting is missing the window where damage can be undone. Once muscle turns to Fibrosis (Scar Tissue), the door to recovery closes.

Phase 1: The Window of Reversibility (The "Now")

Your heart wall is thickened (LVH), but it is still Muscle. Targeted treatment can "thin" that wall back down, restoring your heart's efficiency.

Phase 2: The Window of Stiffness (The Warning)

Arteries become brittle. The "Sandpaper" effect of sugar creates permanent vascular stiffness. Reversibility drops significantly.

Phase 3: The Point of No Return (Permanent Scarring)

Muscle is replaced by Fibrosis. Scar tissue does not pump or stretch. Structural failure is now a permanent fixture of your health.

Structural Health FAQ

Can heart thickening (LVH) actually be reversed?

Yes. During the "Golden Timeline," specific medications like ARBs can signal the heart to stop remodeling and begin thinning the excess muscle wall back to a healthy state.

Why is the Morning Stroke Surge so high-risk?

Your body spikes adrenaline between 6 AM and 10 AM to wake you up. In a remodeled heart, this pressure surge can cause vessel rupture or plaque instability.

How is a Structural Audit different from a GP checkup?

A standard checkup looks at the cuff pressure (the number). Our audit uses high-definition Echocardiograms to see the physical damage to the muscle and valves.
