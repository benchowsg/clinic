The Problem: The "Silent Remodeling" Mask

Why are 1 in 2 Singaporeans undiagnosed? Because the body is incredibly resilient. When your internal pressure rises, the heart muscle thickens (remodels) to handle the load—similar to how a muscle grows at the gym. This is called Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (LVH).

[Image of left ventricular hypertrophy vs normal heart]

The Trap: Because your heart "buffs up" to keep you functioning, you feel perfectly normal. But a "thick" heart is a stiff heart. It is the leading precursor to heart failure among high-functioning professionals in Singapore.

Clinical Audit: Frequently Asked Questions

"I feel 100% fit. Why should I worry about a 140/90 reading?"

High-functioning individuals often have a high 'physiological reserve.' Your body is so efficient at compensating that it masks the damage. However, every minute your pressure is high, your arteries are undergoing Vascular Remodeling—becoming thicker and stiffer. You don't feel the remodeling; you only feel the eventual failure.

"What is Pulse Pressure, and why does Dr. Thang look at it?"

Pulse Pressure is the difference between your Top (Systolic) and Bottom (Diastolic) numbers. A wide gap (e.g., 150/70) often signals Arterial Stiffening. It means your 'pipes' have lost their elastic bounce and are now behaving like stiff tubes, which puts massive strain on the brain’s micro-vessels.

"Is 'White Coat Hypertension' just stress, or a real risk?"

If your blood pressure only spikes at the clinic, it indicates your vascular system is 'hyper-reactive.' A Structural Audit is required to see if these spikes have already caused your heart wall to thicken. If the heart is normal, we manage stress; if it's thick, the spikes are revealing a deeper underlying issue.

"Can I reverse heart thickening?"

Yes. Once identified, medication and lifestyle shifts act as 'scaffolding' to allow the heart muscle to return to a healthy thickness. We only know if it's working by re-auditing the structure, not just watching the numbers on a cuff.
