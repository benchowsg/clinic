Human Growth Hormone (Somatotropin): Beyond Growth

Written & Reviewed by Dr. Gerald Thang

Principal Physician

MBBS (Singapore), MRCP (UK), Graduate Diploma in Cardiology (Singapore)

Human Growth Hormone (HGH), or Somatotropin, is a master regulatory protein naturally produced by the pituitary gland. While widely known for height growth in children, its clinical importance in adults—particularly in metabolic health, heart function, and post-trauma recovery—is a cornerstone of modern regenerative medicine.

The Signs of HGH Deficiency

As we age, HGH production naturally declines. Clinical deficiency can manifest as:

  • Metabolic Shifts: Increased abdominal fat and difficulty losing weight.
  • Cardiac Stress: Elevated LDL cholesterol and triglycerides.
  • Physical Decline: Reduced muscle mass and decreased bone density.

Clinical Roles in Intermediate Care

We frame HGH therapy within our Intermediate Care Framework to ensure systemic recovery:

1. Cardiovascular Stability: HGH maintains the structure and contractility of the heart muscle, supporting long-term cardiac baseline stability.
2. Post-Trauma Recovery: HGH acts as a catalyst for tissue repair and protein synthesis during the transition from hospital to home.
3. Metabolic Efficiency: By stimulating lipolysis, HGH helps manage visceral fat and improves insulin sensitivity.
