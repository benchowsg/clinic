The Health Kick Paradox: Why Cholesterol Rises Despite Your Efforts

By Dr. Gerald Thang

Principal Physician

MBBS (Singapore), MRCP (UK), Graduate Diploma in Cardiology

You’ve started running more miles. You’ve swapped the hawker staples for salads. Yet, your latest lipid profile shows your LDL has climbed significantly. If you are doing "everything right" but the numbers are moving the wrong way, you aren't failing but you are likely dealing with a Metabolic Lock.

1. The "Why": Understanding the Metabolic Lock

When weight increases and LDL spikes despite a "health kick," it usually isn't a lack of willpower. It’s a physiological stalemate caused by three key factors:

  • The Stress Response: Sudden, intense exercise can trigger a cortisol spike. For some, this stress signal tells the body to protect its energy stores, causing it to hold onto visceral fat instead of burning it.
  • The "Hidden Fire": Active visceral fat (the deep yellow fat layer) pumps out inflammatory chemicals. This inflammation "scuffs" your arterial walls, making it physically harder for your body to clear cholesterol from your bloodstream.
  • The Genetic Floor: In some patients, the liver reacts to weight fluctuations by overproducing cholesterol as a survival mechanism, regardless of how "clean" the diet is.
