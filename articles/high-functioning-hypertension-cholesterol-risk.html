The 20-Year Cascade: A Timeline of Erosion

Chronic conditions like Hypertension and Cholesterol don't appear overnight; they are the result of a long-term trajectory.

Ages 20–39: The Foundation

Metabolic flexibility is high, but "Silent Silt" (early cholesterol) begins to coat arterial walls.

Ages 40–59: The Red Zone

Hypertension prevalence triples in Singapore. Organ damage (kidney and heart strain) begins silently.

Ages 60+: The Event Horizon

Cumulative damage leads to clinical events: Strokes, Heart Attacks, or Chronic Kidney Disease.
