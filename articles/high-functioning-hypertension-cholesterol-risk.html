The High-Functioning "Red Zone" Audit

Is your body masking a silent vascular crisis? Complete the 3-step triage below.

1. Does your family history include Heart Attack, Stroke, or High Cholesterol?

2. Do you experience "Success Tax" signs? (Morning headaches, Tinnitus, or Brain Fog)

3. Are you currently between the ages of 40 and 59?

Perspective: The Genetic Infrastructure Risk

Why DNA Overrides Diet

Your history suggests your risk is internal. In many Singaporeans, the liver is genetically programmed to overproduce LDL (sticky cholesterol) regardless of weight. This acts as "Silent Sandblasting" for your heart and kidneys.

The Impact of Being "High-Functioning"

Because you are capable of maintaining a high-stress lifestyle, your body is incredibly good at compensating. However, this lead to Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (LVH)—a thickening of the heart wall that increases the risk of sudden events.

The 20-Year Cascade: A Timeline of Erosion

Chronic conditions like Hypertension, Cholesterol, and Diabetes don't happen overnight. They follow a predictable trajectory:

Ages 25–39: The Foundation

Metabolic flexibility is high, but "Silent Silt" (early LDL) begins to coat arterial walls if a sedentary baseline is set.

Ages 40–59: The "Red Zone"

Hypertension prevalence triples. The body loses its ability to buffer sodium and stress, leading to early End-Organ Damage (kidney strain and heart thickening).

Ages 60+: The Event Horizon

Cumulative damage manifests as clinical events: Strokes, Heart Attacks, or Chronic Kidney Disease.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I have high cholesterol if I am thin and active?

Yes. Genetic high cholesterol (Familial Hypercholesterolemia) is common in Singapore. Your liver may overproduce LDL regardless of your lifestyle.

Why is age 40 considered the 'Red Zone'?

Clinical data shows that the cumulative effect of genetics and work-life stress typically crosses the threshold into hypertension and metabolic syndrome starting at age 40.

What is an Infrastructure Audit?

It goes beyond a blood test. It measures the structural health of your heart and the stiffness of your arteries to see how your body is actually coping with stress.
