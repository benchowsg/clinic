The 20-Year Disease Cascade

Vascular damage is cumulative. The events people face in their 60s are often set in motion during their 40s. It is preventable and managable when detected and acted upon during this critical window.

Ages 20–39: Foundation

Silent accumulation of LDL (cholesterol) begins to coat arterial walls.

Ages 40–59: The Red Zone

Hypertension prevalence triples. The heart and kidneys begin to show early signs of strain.

Ages 60+: The Event Horizon

Cumulative structural damage manifests as clinical events like strokes or heart attacks.

Vascular Health: Frequently Asked Questions

Why is it called "High-Functioning" Hypertension?

Many of us are working adults maintaining high productivity at work despite dangerously high blood pressure. Because the body is resilient, it compensates by thickening the heart wall, effectively "hiding" the problem until a major event occurs.

Can I have high cholesterol if I am thin and active?

Yes. This is often "Genetic High Cholesterol." Your liver may be genetically programmed to overproduce LDL regardless of your body fat percentage or exercise routine. Only a clinical audit can reveal this internal risk.

What is a Heart-Related Screening vs. a regular blood test?

A regular blood test tells you what is in your blood today. A Heart-Related Screening uses ultrasound and stiffness measurements to see if your organs have already been damaged by years of silent pressure.

Why is age 40 to 59 called the Red Zone for heart health?

In Singapore, hypertension prevalence triples between ages 40 and 59. This is the period where metabolic buffering fails and silent vascular erosion accelerates.

If my BP is 180/120 but my heart rate is normal, am I safe?

No. A normal heart rate does not negate the danger of a Hypertensive Crisis. High pressure alone can damage small blood vessels in the brain and kidneys. If your BP is 180/120 mmHg or higher, rest for 5 minutes and retest. If it remains high, contact a medical professional immediately, regardless of your heart rate.
