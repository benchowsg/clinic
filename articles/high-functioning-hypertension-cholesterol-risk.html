The 20-Year Disease Cascade

Vascular damage is cumulative. The events people face in their 60s are often set in motion during their 40s.

Ages 20–39: Foundation

Silent accumulation of LDL (cholesterol) begins to coat arterial walls.

Ages 40–59: The Red Zone

Hypertension prevalence triples. The heart and kidneys begin to show early signs of "Success Tax" strain.

Ages 60+: The Event Horizon

Cumulative structural damage manifests as clinical events like Strokes or Heart Attacks.

Vascular Health: Frequently Asked Questions

Why is it called "High-Functioning" Hypertension?

Many successful professionals maintain high productivity despite dangerously high blood pressure. Because the body is resilient, it compensates by thickening the heart wall, effectively "hiding" the problem until a major event occurs.

Can I have high cholesterol if I am thin and active?

Yes. This is often "Genetic High Cholesterol." Your liver may be genetically programmed to overproduce LDL regardless of your body fat percentage or exercise routine. Only a clinical audit can reveal this internal risk.

What is a Heart-Related Screening vs. a regular blood test?

A regular blood test tells you what is in your blood today. A Heart-Related Screening uses ultrasound and stiffness measurements to see if your organs have already been damaged by years of silent pressure.
