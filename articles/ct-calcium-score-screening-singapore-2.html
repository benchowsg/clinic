The Calcium Score: Why a Specialist’s Interpretation Matters More than the Scan

By Dr. Gerald Thang

Cardiovascular Stewardship

MBBS (Singapore), MRCP (UK), Graduate Diploma in Cardiology

"I went for a heart scan at a generic screening center. They gave me a number and told me to see a doctor. But no one told me if I was safe or if I needed to worry."

A CT Calcium Score is a snapshot. Stewardship is the movie. In my clinic, we don't just give you a number; we give you a biological roadmap.

In Singapore, many patients assume all heart scans are equal. However, receiving a report from a laboratory is very different from a Clinical Audit by a cardiologist. A lab report tells you the "what"; Stewardship tells you the "why" and the "what next."

The "MESA" Difference: Your Biological Age

A score of 100 might be expected for an 80-year-old, but for a 45-year-old executive, it is a metabolic emergency. At A Healing Heart, we don't just look at the raw Agatston Score. We use the MESA (Multi-Ethnic Study of Atherosclerosis) percentile database to determine your true heart age.

Is your heart older than you are?

By adjusting your score for your age, gender, and ethnicity, we determine your Arterial Age. If your IC says 40 but your heart is 65, our goal is to "freeze" that progression immediately through targeted load reduction.

Why "Normal" Stress Tests Can Be Dangerous

The biggest myth in Singapore heart health is that a "passed" Treadmill Stress Test means you are safe. This is factually incorrect for early prevention. A stress test only identifies late-stage blockages, whereas the Calcium Score identifies the "silent" risk before symptoms begin.

The Thang Method: Following Through

If you have plaque, the question isn't just "how much," but "why is it forming?" This is where my role as your Vascular Steward begins. We follow through by auditing your "invisible" risks:

  • Lipid Audit (ApoB/Lp(a)): Is your cholesterol "sticky" or inflammatory? Understand why your cholesterol might stay high despite diet.
  • Immune Friction: Are undiagnosed allergies or sensitivities acting as "vascular sandpaper" on your arteries?
  • Metabolic Resistance: Is hidden Metabolic Syndrome driving your plaque growth?

Putting the "Fire" out

Calcium is like the scar tissue left behind by a fire (inflammation). A generic screening center tells you there is a scar. My job is to ensure the fire is out. We use the scan as a baseline to measure our success in stabilizing your vascular health.

Who is the right fit for this audit?

I recommend a specialist-led Calcium Score Audit for:

  • Individuals with a family history of "sudden" heart issues.
  • Anyone on the fence about starting long-term statin therapy.
  • Executives who need the "Power of Zero"—the clinical peace of mind that comes from a perfect score.
  • Those noticing early metabolic warning signals like hair thinning.

A final thought: In cardiology, the most expensive scan is the one that gives you data without a plan. True prevention requires a specialist to translate that data into life-years.
