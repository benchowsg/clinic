The Calcium Score: Why the Right Interpretation Matters More than the Scan

By Dr. Gerald Thang

Cardiovascular Stewardship

MBBS (Singapore), MRCP (UK), Graduate Diploma in Cardiology

"I went for a heart scan at a generic screening center. They gave me a number and told me to see a doctor. But no one told me if I was safe or if I needed to worry."

A CT Calcium Score is a snapshot. Stewardship is the movie. In my clinic, we don't just give you a number; we give you a biological roadmap.

In Singapore, many patients assume all heart scans are equal. However, receiving a report from a laboratory is very different from a Clinical Audit by a trained doctor in the field of cardiology. A lab report tells you the "what"; Stewardship tells you the "why" and the "what next."

The "MESA" Difference: Your Biological Age

A score of 100 might be expected for an 80-year-old, but for a 45-year-old executive, it is a metabolic emergency. At A Healing Heart, we don't just look at the raw Agatston Score. We use the MESA (Multi-Ethnic Study of Atherosclerosis) percentile database to determine your true heart age.

Is your heart older than you are?

By adjusting your score for your age, gender, and ethnicity, we determine your Arterial Age. If your IC says 40 but your heart is 65, our goal is to "freeze" that progression immediately through targeted load reduction.

Why a CT Calcium Score is Necessary

Most heart attacks in Singapore occur in individuals who consider themselves "healthy." A standard health screening might show normal cholesterol and a passing treadmill test, but these are lagging indicators. They only show trouble once the "pipe" is already failing.

A CT Calcium Score is a leading indicator. It allows us to see inside the arterial wall years before a blockage occurs. We aren't just looking for a number; we are looking for the presence of sub-clinical atherosclerosis.

What are we actually finding?

When we perform this scan, we are identifying the "Biological Footprint" of your lifestyle and genetics. Here is what the scan reveals:

The Plaque Burden

We are measuring the total volume of calcified plaque in your coronary arteries. This isn't just "fat"; it's a hardened substance that indicates your body has been in a chronic state of inflammatory repair.

Arterial Ageing

The scan reveals if your heart is ageing faster than the rest of your body. We find the "Biological Age" of your vessels, which helps us understand your 10-year risk of a cardiovascular event.

The "Silent" Plaque Discovery

The most critical finding is often Silent Plaque. This is plaque that hasn't caused a blockage yet, so it wouldn't show up on a stress test or cause chest pain. However, this plaque can be "unstable."

By finding this early, we can identify if you have Metabolic Syndrome or endogenous cholesterol issues that are actively "scarring" your arteries. The scan tells us exactly where the damage is starting, so we can stop the "rusting" process before it narrows the vessel.

The "Missed" 50%

Research shows that 50% of all heart attacks occur in people with normal LDL cholesterol levels. The CT Calcium scan is the only non-invasive way to find the plaque that the blood test misses. We are finding the actual disease, not just the risk factor.

Why Treadmill (Exercise) Stress Tests Can Be Dangerous

The biggest myth in Singapore heart health is that a "passed" Treadmill Stress Test means you are safe. This is factually incorrect for early prevention. But it is not for everyone. A stress test only identifies late-stage blockages, whereas the Calcium Score identifies the "silent" risk before symptoms begin.

Putting the "Fire" out

Calcium is like the scar tissue left behind by a fire (inflammation). A generic screening center tells you there is a scar. My job is to ensure the fire is out. We use the scan as a baseline to measure our success in stabilizing your vascular health.
