Are you wasting your supplements? A Guide for the Health Conscious

By Dr. Gerald Thang

Principal Physician

MBBS (Singapore), MRCP (UK), Graduate Diploma in Cardiology (Singapore)

We often treat supplements as a "set it and forget it" addition to our health routine. However, the human gut is a busy "transport" hub. Some vitamins act as "team players" that help each other absorb, while others are "rivals" that fight for the same entry points.

At A Healing Heart Medical, our goal is to ensure that the supplements you take are clinically effective, rather than simply passing through your system unused.

Iron Supplements

If you take Iron tablets to support metabolism but wash them down with your morning Kopi or Teh (coffee or tea), you may be blocking up to 90% of the absorption. Tannins in caffeine act like a lock, preventing iron from entering your system. To fix your fatigue, your iron needs a 3-hour head start before drink your first caffeine.

Vitamin D Supplements

Living on a sunny island doesn't guarantee Vitamin D levels. In fact, many of our high-performing patients are clinically deficient. But the real waste? Taking Vitamin D with just water. As a fat-soluble nutrient, it requires a "carrier", like eggs or avocado, to reach your heart and bones. Without it, it simply passes through you.

Why "DIY" Supplementing Can Be Risky

At A Healing Heart Medical, we see vitamins as part of your Medication Micromanagement. Taking the wrong supplements at the wrong time doesn't just waste money, it can interfere with chronic medications, such as blood thinners or blood pressure pills.

1. The Mineral Traffic Jam

Calcium and Magnesium use the same pathway. Taking them together means they fight for position, and you absorb less of both. Smart Fix: Calcium for breakfast, Magnesium for bedtime.

2. Find More "Absorption Buddies"

Fat-soluble vitamins like D, E, and K are oils. They cannot dissolve in water. Without a "fatty transport" (like eggs, avocado, or nuts), your body may only absorb a fraction of the dose. The Smart Fix: Pairing Vitamin C with Iron is a "pro-move" to double your intake efficiency.

Clinical Safety & Timing Guide

Use this guide to structure your daily routine for maximum metabolic harmony.

Morning (Empty Stomach)

Iron & Vitamin B12: Best taken immediately upon waking with water or juice. Keep 2 hours away from high-dose Vitamin C.

Breakfast / Lunch (With Food)

Vitamin D & Omega-3: Requires dietary fat.
Zinc: Take after food to prevent nausea.

Nighttime

Magnesium: Helps relax muscles and promotes sleep. Keeps it clear from your morning Calcium dose.
