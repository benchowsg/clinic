Understanding Heart Screening in Singapore: An Educational Guide

Written & Reviewed by Dr. Gerald Thang

MBBS (Singapore), MRCP (UK), Graduate Diploma in Cardiology (Singapore)

Many Singaporeans undergo "screening" as part of their annual work-checkup, but cardiac screening is often more nuanced. The goal of heart screening is to detect subclinical cardiovascular disease—conditions that may not yet show symptoms but pose a risk for the future.

Medical Note: Screening is most effective when tailored to an individual’s risk factors such as age, family history, and lifestyle.

Common Cardiac Assessment Tools

In a clinical setting, several tools are used to assess heart health. Each serves a different diagnostic purpose:

1. Electrocardiogram (ECG)

A basic test that records the electrical activity of your heart. It is useful for detecting arrhythmias (irregular heartbeats) or signs of a previous heart attack.

2. Exercise Stress Test (Treadmill)

This monitors how your heart responds to physical stress. It is often used to detect coronary artery disease that might not be visible while the heart is at rest.

3. Echocardiogram (Heart Ultrasound)

An ultrasound that allows the doctor to see the heart’s structure, including the valves and the pumping strength of the heart chambers.

Who Should Consider Screening?

Under the Healthier SG framework, risk-based screening is encouraged. You should speak with a doctor about a cardiac evaluation if you experience:

  • Persistent chest discomfort or breathlessness.
  • A strong family history of early-onset heart disease.
  • Known chronic conditions like Hypertension or Diabetes.
