Beyond the Report: Why Interpretation is the Most Critical Part of Heart Screening

Written and Reviewed by Dr. Gerald Thang

Principal Physician

MBBS (Singapore), MRCP (UK), Graduate Diploma in Cardiology (Singapore)

In Singapore, heart screening packages often appear identical on the surface. Whether you choose a basic ECG or a high resolution CT Coronary Angiogram, the technology used across accredited clinics is largely standardized. However, the true value of a screening is not found in the raw data or the colorful charts in your medical report: it is found in the clinical interpretation and the subsequent roadmap for your health.

A medical report is a snapshot in time, but your health is a continuous narrative. Without the right lens, a normal result can provide a false sense of security, while a borderline result can cause unnecessary anxiety. The role of the physician is to bridge the gap between what the machine sees and what your body actually needs.

The Principal Physician Perspective

"Data without context is just noise. At A Healing Heart, we believe that screening is the beginning of a conversation, not the end of a transaction. The real work begins after the report is printed: correlating those numbers with your lifestyle, genetics, and long term vitality goals."

The Difference Between Detection and Guidance

Standard screening focuses on Detection: finding out if a disease is currently present. While this is necessary, it is often reactive. Our approach focuses on Guidance and Follow Through. This means looking at the velocity of change in your markers and understanding how different risk factors interact with one another.

For example, a slightly elevated cholesterol level in a patient with high Lp(a) requires a vastly different clinical response than the same level in a patient without that genetic risk. A standard report might flag both as high, but the Principal Physician provides the nuanced strategy required to manage that specific risk profile.

The Importance of Continuity

One of the biggest considerations for anyone choosing a screening provider should be the Continuity of Care. What happens if a minor abnormality is found? In many high volume screening centers, you are simply referred elsewhere. At our clinic, the physician who interprets your results is the same one who manages your follow through. This ensures that your clinical history is never fragmented and your health strategy remains consistent.

Choosing a screening provider is a decision about who you trust to navigate your health journey. Look beyond the equipment and the price point: consider the depth of guidance and the commitment to your long term wellness that the physician provides.
