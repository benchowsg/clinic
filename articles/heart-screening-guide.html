Understanding Heart Screening: A Modern Overview

Written & Reviewed by Dr. Gerald Thang

MBBS (Singapore), MRCP (UK), Graduate Diploma in Cardiology (Singapore)

Heart screening is a proactive step toward identifying potential cardiovascular issues before symptoms arise. In Singapore, modern cardiology utilizes a range of diagnostic tools to assess different aspects of heart health, from the "plumbing" of the arteries to the "electrical" rhythm of the heart.

Commonly Utilized Diagnostic Tools

1. CT Coronary Angiogram (CTCA)

A non-invasive imaging scan that provides a detailed view of the coronary arteries. It is used to identify the presence and severity of arterial narrowings or blockages.

2. Echocardiogram (Heart Ultrasound)

An ultrasound used to evaluate the heart's structure, including chamber size, pumping strength, and valve function.

3. Holter Monitoring

A portable device that records the heart's electrical activity continuously for 24 to 48 hours. This is typically used to capture heart rhythm irregularities (palpitations) that a standard ECG might miss.

4. Electrocardiogram (ECG)

A fundamental tool that records the heart's electrical signals at a single point in time to check for immediate rhythm issues.

5. Exercise Stress Test (Treadmill)

While historically common, the treadmill test is often considered a secondary tool today. It monitors the heart's response to physical exertion but provides less anatomical detail than modern CT imaging.

The Importance of Consultation: Choosing the right screening tool requires a clinical evaluation. Factors such as symptoms, lifestyle, and genetic predisposition determine which assessment provides the most value for your specific situation.
