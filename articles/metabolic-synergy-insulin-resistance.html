Why Insulin (Not Just Blood Sugar) is the Real Heart Concern

By Dr. Gerald Thang

Principal Physician

MBBS (Singapore), MRCP (UK), Graduate Diploma in Cardiology

In my clinic, I often consult with patients who are confused. They have "normal" blood sugar reports, yet their arteries show signs of premature aging. This is the "Normal Glucose Trap."

Research Findings

Landmark Cardiovascular Outcome Trials (CVOTs) have fundamentally changed how we view heart protection. Clinical data confirms that metabolic interventions reduce heart attacks and strokes independently of weight loss or diabetes status. This means that high insulin and metabolic signaling are direct toxic stressors to your vascular lining, and treating them is essential to saving the heart.

Read the SELECT Trial (The New England Journal of Medicine, NEJM, Nov 2023)

What to Look Out For

Insulin resistance is a signal processing problem. Your muscle cells become "hard of hearing," forcing your pancreas to "shout" by producing more insulin. Look for these subtle red flags:

  • The TOFI Phenotype: "Thin on the Outside, Fat on the Inside." Common in Asian biology where visceral fat accumulates around the heart despite a normal BMI.
  • Post-Meal Brain Fog: Feeling lethargic or "crashing" after carbohydrate-heavy meals.
  • Subclinical Hypertension: Blood pressure that creeps up even when you are phyiscally active and eating well.
[Image of visceral fat versus subcutaneous fat]

The Life Stage & Genetic Factor

Vulnerability to insulin resistance isn't just about diet; it’s about your Familial Timeline:

  • The Genetic Headstart: If heart disease or diabetes runs in your family, your "Silent Decade" of vascular stress likely starts early.
  • Hormonal Shifts: In menopause or andropause, the loss of protective hormones acts as a "Metabolic Lock," accelerating arterial stiffening.

Can Lifestyle Changes Work?

Yes, but they must be targeted. Generic caloric restriction often fails because it doesn't improve Energy Partitioning. We focus on Zone 2 cardio and protein-first nutrition to restore cellular "hearing." However, when the risk of a cardiovascular event is high, we use metabolic medication as a clinical bridge to protect the arteries during your recovery.

Prevention & Recovery: It is Not a Death Sentence

I want my patients to understand that metabolic dysfunction is a reversible state. We focus on restoration—returning your vascular system to a state of elasticity and allowing your pancreas to go back to a "whisper."

The Path Forward: How to Go About It

If you are concerned about your family history or have noticed a weight plateau, we move upstream with a Structural Audit:

  • Step 1: Metabolic Profiling. We look at Fasting Insulin and HOMA-IR, not just average blood sugar (HbA1c).
  • Step 2: Vascular Mapping. We check for subclinical arterial stiffening.
  • Step 3: Restoration Plan. A clinical roadmap combining precision lifestyle shifts and heart-protective medical therapy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you have heart disease with normal blood sugar?

Yes. Hyperinsulinemia damages the heart for years before blood sugar ever rises. We see this frequently in the "TOFI" phenotype common in Singapore.

Is heart disease inevitable if it's in my family history?

No. Genetics may load the gun, but the metabolic environment pulls the trigger. By fixing your insulin sensitivity, you can effectively bypass the familial timeline.
