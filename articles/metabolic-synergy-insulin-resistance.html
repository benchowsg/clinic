The Silent Driver: Why Insulin Resistance is a Cardiology Concern

By Dr. Gerald Thang

Principal Physician

MBBS (Singapore), MRCP (UK), Graduate Diploma in Cardiology (Singapore)

By the time your fasting glucose rises, the vascular conversation has already been happening for a decade. In clinical practice, I often encounter patients with "perfect" blood sugar reports who are nevertheless trending toward a cardiovascular event.

The culprit is often Insulin Resistance. It's a state where the body’s cells stop responding effectively to insulin, forcing the pancreas to pump out higher volumes to maintain equilibrium. This state of hyperinsulinemia is a direct driver of arterial stiffening.

The "Normal" Glucose Trap

Standard health screenings typically rely on Fasting Glucose or HbA1c. While useful, these are lagging indicators. They only show a "fail" once the body’s compensatory mechanisms can no longer keep up with the metabolic demand. In contrast, circulating insulin levels are a leading indicator.

The Hyperinsulinemia Factor

A patient can maintain normal blood sugar for years while having dangerously high insulin. This excess insulin acts as a growth factor for the smooth muscle cells in your arterial walls, causing them to thicken and harden long before diabetes is ever diagnosed.

The Vascular Cost of Metabolic Dysfunction

High circulating insulin is not benign; it is a pro-inflammatory signal that irritates the delicate endothelial lining of the heart. This contributes to three major cardiovascular risks:

  • Arterial Stiffening: Reduced vessel elasticity that forces the heart to work harder to maintain circulation.
  • Sodium Retention: High insulin signals the kidneys to hold onto salt, driving up baseline blood pressure.
  • Plaque Acceleration: It creates an environment where LDL cholesterol is more likely to oxidize and stick to vessel walls.

Clinical Strategy: Moving Upstream

Protecting your heart requires us to achieve Metabolic Synergy. Our approach moves beyond standard biomarkers to include:

  • HOMA-IR Assessment: Measuring the relationship between glucose and insulin to find the real biological baseline.
  • Vascular Health Mapping: Correlating your metabolic data with the actual physical state of your arteries.
  • Personalized Restoration: Using precision nutrition and targeted medical therapy to restore insulin sensitivity and protect the endothelium.

The Principal Physician's Note

Cardiology is no longer just about plumbing; it is about the metabolic environment in which your heart exists. By identifying insulin resistance early, we aren't just preventing diabetes; we are halting the premature aging of your vascular system.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you have normal glucose but high insulin?

Yes. This is known as hyperinsulinemia. The body overproduces insulin to keep blood sugar stable, masking metabolic dysfunction that can damage the arteries for years before diabetes is diagnosed.

Is HbA1c enough to detect heart risk?

HbA1c is a lagging indicator that shows average blood sugar over three months. In our clinical view, relying solely on HbA1c can be misleading, as insulin resistance often damages the cardiovascular system for years before blood sugar levels actually rise. It is better to combine routine fasting glucose test for early prevention.
