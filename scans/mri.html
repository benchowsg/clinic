Cardiac MRI: The Gold Standard

Cardiac Magnetic Resonance (CMR) is the most sophisticated non-invasive tool in modern cardiology. It allows us to differentiate between healthy tissue, inflammation, and permanent scarring with unparalleled precision.

Tissue Characterization & Scarring

The hallmark of Cardiac MRI is its ability to perform Late Gadolinium Enhancement (LGE). This technique identifies areas of the heart muscle that have been replaced by fibrous scar tissue.

For metabolic patients, this is vital. It can reveal "silent" heart attacks or chronic inflammation (myocarditis) that may be the underlying cause of unexplained fatigue or shortness of breath.

🧲

Detecting Silent Myocardial Scarring

Myocardial Viability Testing

If a part of the heart isn't moving well, we need to know if it's "dead" or just "hibernating" due to poor blood flow. MRI is the only tool that can definitively tell us if the muscle is viable.

This information is the deciding factor in whether a patient will benefit from a procedure like angioplasty or if medical management is the safer route.

Clinical Decision Tool

"MRI removes the guesswork. We don't just see that a wall is weak; we see why it's weak. This precision prevents unnecessary surgeries and focuses treatment where it will actually work."

Preparation & Safety

The Experience

The scan typically takes 45–60 minutes. You will lie inside a tunnel-shaped scanner. Because the machine uses magnetic resonance, it generates rhythmic knocking sounds; we provide headphones and music for your comfort.

Contraindications

Due to the strong magnetic field, patients with certain metal implants, older pacemakers, or metallic fragments in the eye may not be suitable. We conduct a rigorous safety screening for every patient before the scan.
