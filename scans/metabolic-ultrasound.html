Metabolic Support Imaging: Beyond the Scale

Heart health is a systemic issue. We focus on the organs (Liver, Kidneys, and Vascular tree) that drive your metabolism to identify the "silent" markers of metabolic stress that standard blood tests often miss.

Hepatobiliary (HBS) Scan: The Metabolic Engine

The liver is your primary site for glucose and lipid processing. Metabolic Associated Fatty Liver Disease (MAFLD) is often the first sign of insulin resistance. Our HBS scans visualize fatty infiltration (steatosis) and structural changes, allowing for intervention long before it leads to permanent scarring or heart complications.

Carotid Intima-Media Thickness (CIMT)

The CIMT scan is one of our most proactive metabolic markers. It measures the thickness of the inner layers of the carotid arteries in the neck. Even without a visible blockage, a thickened "Intima-Media" layer indicates that your arteries are aging faster than your chronological age—often due to chronic insulin or cholesterol stress.

Renal (KUB) & Kidney Doppler

High blood sugar and chronic pressure place immense strain on the kidneys' delicate filtration system. We utilize a Kidney & Urinary Bladder (KUB) scan combined with Doppler visualization to assess the structural integrity of the kidneys.

While the KUB scan identifies stones, cysts, or structural changes, the Doppler component allows us to observe the vascular perfusion within the kidney tissue. This helps Dr. Gerald identify early signs of kidney strain caused by metabolic imbalance before it affects your long-term renal function.
