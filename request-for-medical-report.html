Request for Medical Report

Patients can request a medical report in person at the clinic's registration counter and submit the required supporting documents. They can also post the supporting documents by mail.


Fees for Each Request

We charge each request for a medical report. The medical report fee may include additional charges such as X-ray and Laboratory Investigation.

You can pay:

  • In person at the clinic's registration counter or
  • Via bank transfer (PayNow) to UEN 202113244D. Please include the patient's full name for reference.

Please note that Medisave cannot be used to pay for the medical report.

There is no refund once payment is made.


Processing Time for Each Request

Each request is subjected to A Healing Heart Medical Pte. Ltd.'s approval. We will process application only after receiving the necessary supporting documents and payment.

The process will take at least 7 working days.


Required Supporting Documents

For us to process your application, we will require the following supporting documents:

  1. A copy of the patient's NRIC / FIN / Passport / or identification documents.
  2. The patient's consent to release his/her medical information.
    • (A) For patient who is requesting for personal use, please download and complete the application form.
    • (B) For patient who is requesting for third party use (e.g., insurance claim, work-related injury, legal matters), please kindly attach (A) and also seek the third party agent to produce the letter of authorisation with the patient's signatory, date, name, and ID number. The letter must include the company logo /letterhead /stamp. It must state the purpose for releasing patient's medical information.

In addition, please note the following:

  1. Patient who is below 21 years old, please seek consent of the patient's parents or legal guardian.
  2. Deceased patient or patient who is unable to give consent due to medical conditions, please seek consent from the appointed representative of patient's estate.
  3. Additional supporting documents are required to proof representative's relationship to patient including but not limited to death certificate, birth certificate, passport (for foreigners), marriage certificate, letter of authorisation, Grant of Probate, Lasting Power of Attorney.
  4. The date of the consent must be clearly written together with the patient's signatory, name and ID number. The validity of the consent given is six months. If the consent was signed more than six months ago, the patient / parent / legal guardian / legal representative will need to sign a new application form.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why do I need to request for my own medical report?

    Submitting an application or consent form to release medical records is necessary because of patient privacy, confidentiality, and compliance with legal regulations. Medical records contain sensitive and personal information about a patient's health, medical history, and treatment details. Releasing such information without proper consent could lead to breaches of privacy and compromise the confidentiality of the patient's data.

    Requiring patients to submit an application for the release of medical records ensures that they are fully informed about the process. By signing the consent form, patients acknowledge that they understand what information will be shared, to whom it will be disclosed, and for what purpose.

  • How do I prevent unauthorised access?

    Without the formal application or consent, we will never disclose any information to your employer or any third party agency. Requiring a formal application or consent helps prevent unauthorised access to your medical records. It ensures that only authorised individuals, such as the patient or their authorised representatives, have the right to access the information.

  • Why does the clinic require such application?

    Keeping a record of the consent forms helps the clinic maintain a transparent and accountable system for managing medical records requests. It ensures that all requests are legitimate, properly documented, and handled appropriately.

    Requiring consent protects the clinic from potential liability issues and malpractices claims. It demonstrates our committment to take the necessary steps to comply with regulations and respect patient rights.

    By obtaining patient consent through a formal application process, we can assure patients that we act ethically and legally while respecting patient autonomy and privacy. This process is necessary to foster trust between patient and the clinic, as patients are more confident that their sensitive medical information will be handled with care and confidentiality.
