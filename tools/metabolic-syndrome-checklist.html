Understanding Why These Specific Markers

Why use Fasting Glucose vs. HbA1c?

While HbA1c shows your average sugar over 3 months, Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG) is an "early warning" marker. In Syndrome X, FPG often rises first because the liver becomes resistant to insulin overnight. Catching a high FPG early allows us to intervene before your HbA1c reaches the diabetic range.

How to measure Waist Circumference?

This is not your "pant size." Stand upright and wrap a tape measure around your waist, midway between the bottom of your ribs and the top of your hip bone (usually right across the belly button). For the Asian population, abdominal fat is a more significant predictor of heart disease than total weight.

What makes Triglycerides high?

Triglycerides are fats in your blood often driven by a diet high in refined carbohydrates, sugar, and alcohol. When your body has more energy than it needs, it converts those calories into triglycerides to store in fat cells. High levels contribute to the hardening of your arteries.

Is Syndrome X reversible?

Yes. Unlike many chronic conditions, Metabolic Syndrome is a "warning phase." Through personalized medical management, muscle-centric exercise, and nutritional adjustments, you can reverse these markers and return your cardiovascular risk to a baseline level.
