The "Asian Phenotype" & Metabolic Risk

In Singapore, a "normal" appearance can be deceiving. Research confirms that Asian populations face significantly higher risks of heart disease and diabetes at a lower Body Mass Index (BMI) compared to Western populations.

The "TOFI" Profile

"Thin-on-the-Outside, Fat-on-the-Inside." Many Singaporeans (Asian Ethnicity) carry visceral fat (fat around internal organs) despite having slim limbs, which triggers early arterial inflammation.

Ethnic Sensitivity (2026 Data)

South Asian/Indian individuals often have higher levels of Lp(a), a genetic protein that accelerates blockages. Malay and Chinese profiles show higher sensitivity to salt and refined carbohydrates, leading to earlier onset of hypertension.

Which number to enter?
Use the "Top Number"

The Systolic reading is the pressure when your heart beats. It is the most critical number for 2026 heart health tracking.

Tip: Use an upper-arm cuff. Rest for 5 minutes before taking the reading for an accurate 30-year trajectory score.

Standard Units

mmol/L: The standard used in Singapore (e.g., 5.2).

mg/dL: Used in the US or some fitness apps (e.g., 200).
