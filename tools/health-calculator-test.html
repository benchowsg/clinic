Why Ethnicity & BMI Matter

Medical research in Singapore indicates that Asian populations face higher metabolic risks at a lower BMI compared to Western populations. This is largely attributed to the "Asian Phenotype", which has a tendency to carry higher visceral (internal) fat even with a seemingly slim exterior.

Prevalence Insights (Singapore Context)

South Asian / Indian: Highest prevalence of diabetes (14.2%) and multi-vessel CAD risk. Genetic high Lp(a) is more common in this group.

Malay: Elevated rates of hypertension and central obesity, with a diabetes prevalence of approx. 14.4%.

Chinese: While diabetes prevalence is 8.2%, cardiovascular events spike rapidly once BMI hits 23.0 due to localized visceral fat accumulation.

Asian BMI Thresholds

Health risks for Asians escalate at a BMI of 23.0, whereas Western standards often don't flag risk until 25.0.

Prevalence & Genetic Risk

Singapore’s 2025 health landscape emphasizes early genetic screening for **Familial Hypercholesterolaemia (FH)**. With diabetes prevalence remaining high among South Asians (14.2%) and Malays (14.4%), the 2026 guidelines move away from waiting for "damage" to occur, opting instead for early aggressive lipid management starting in the 30s.
