Beyond the Numbers: Achieving True Metabolic Synergy

Moving away from chasing biomarkers toward high-resolution vascular health and preventive optimization.

Written & Reviewed by Dr. Gerald Thang

Principal Physician

MBBS (Singapore), MRCP (UK), Graduate Diploma in Cardiology (Singapore)

In many standard health screenings across Singapore, a patient is told they are "healthy" if their BMI is under 23 and their LDL cholesterol is under 130. However, current clinical data reveals a startling reality: nearly 50% of individuals who suffer heart attacks have 'normal' cholesterol levels.

At A Healing Heart Medical, we believe that basic metrics often provide a false sense of security. To achieve true longevity, we must look at the "Hidden Fire" of metabolic health: Visceral Adiposity and Particle Concentration.

1. The 'Skinny-Fat' Paradox: The Rule of 0.5

Asian biology is unique. We tend to develop metabolic complications at a much lower BMI than Westerners. This is the TOFI phenotype (Thin-on-the-Outside, Fat-on-the-Inside). You may look lean, but your internal organs may be "suffocating" under visceral fat.

The Clinical Metric: We prioritize the Waist-to-Height Ratio (WtHR) over BMI. The goal is simple: Keep your waist circumference to less than half your height.

Crossing the 0.5 threshold is a biological tipping point. It indicates that your safe storage areas are full, and fat is now leaking into your liver and heart, triggering systemic inflammation that "scuffs" your arterial walls.

2. ApoB: Counting the 'Burglars' at the Door

Standard lipid panels measure the weight of your cholesterol (LDL-C). But weight doesn't cause blockages; particles do.

We utilize Apolipoprotein B (ApoB) testing to get a high-resolution head-count of every potentially plaque-forming particle in your blood.

  • The Analogy: LDL-C is like weighing a suitcase. ApoB is counting the number of suitcases. It is the number of particles that determines how many will eventually burrow into your artery walls.
  • The Strategy: By lowering your ApoB count, we effectively reduce the "traffic" in your arteries, regardless of what your total cholesterol weight says.

3. The 24-Hour Shield: Achieving Steady-State Safety

Metabolic synergy isn't about a one-time "clean" report; it is about maintaining Steady-State protection. Whether it is through chronotherapy (timing medication to prevent morning BP surges) or addressing insulin resistance, our goal is to eliminate the "peaks" of risk that lead to vascular burnout.
