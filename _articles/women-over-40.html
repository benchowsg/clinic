The 5-Year Critical Zone

Clinical data confirms that a woman's cardiovascular risk accelerates exponentially in the five years surrounding the final menstrual period. This is your most critical window for metabolic intervention.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I have heart issues if my blood pressure is normal?

Yes. Women often develop microvascular disease (stiffening of the tiny vessels), which may not show up on a standard BP cuff but can cause breathlessness and fatigue.

Is the weight gain after 40 strictly due to diet?

No. It is primarily hormonal. As estrogen drops, the body becomes resistant to insulin, signaling the body to store fat around the organs (visceral fat) even if your calorie intake hasn't changed.

Is HRT enough to protect my heart and thyroid?

HRT is a powerful tool for vascular protection, but it is not a "magic bullet." It must be paired with metabolic stewardship. An audit ensures that your insulin sensitivity and lipid profiles are optimized so that the HRT can work effectively without being hindered by underlying metabolic resistance.

Can I start HRT if I have a family history of heart disease?

This is exactly why a pre-HRT Cardiac Audit is essential. By using Echocardiography (Echo) and advanced lipid screening, we can establish your baseline safety profile. For many women, starting HRT early in the "Critical Window" (Phase 2) actually provides significant long-term protection against the heart disease they may be genetically predisposed to.
