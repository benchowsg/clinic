Cardiac Screening: Clinical FAQ

Understanding advanced diagnostics for heart disease prevention.

What is the difference between an ECG and an Echocardiogram?

An ECG measures the electrical activity of your heart to detect rhythm issues. An Echocardiogram is an ultrasound that looks at the physical structure, valves, and pumping strength of the heart muscle.

When should I consider a CT Calcium Score?

We recommend a CT Calcium Score if you have risk factors like high cholesterol or a family history of heart disease. It is the "Gold Standard" for detecting silent plaque buildup before a heart attack occurs.

How does 15-minute rapid blood analysis help?
