Who is most at risk?

Bone health is a silent priority as we age

Women (Post-Menopause)

Hormonal changes after menopause cause bone density to drop rapidly. This is the most critical time to start "locking in" bone strength.

Men & Seniors (Age 65+)

As men age, their testosterone level declines, coupled with other chronic issues from long-term smoking, stomach acid or prostate issues, these can weaken the bone.

The Warning Signs

Osteoporosis is often called the "Silent Thief" because you can't feel bones thinning. However, look out for these physical clues:

  • Loss of Height: Noticing you are 2cm or more shorter than you used to be.
  • Change in Posture: A "stoop" or curving in the upper back.
  • Weakened Grip: Finding it harder to open jars or hold heavy objects.
  • The "Easy" Break: A fracture caused by a minor trip or even a strong sneeze.
