Who is most vulnerable?

Men and Women (Age 30 to 45)

This is the 'Peak Bone' phase. If you have low activity (no exercise) or poor nutrition, you may be missing the chance to build a strong "bone bank" before the natural decline starts.

Women (Post-Menopause)

Hormonal changes cause density to drop rapidly. This is the critical window to 'lock in' strength before the bone mesh becomes too porous.

Men & Seniors (Age 65+)

Men are often diagnosed too late. Dropping testosterone, prostate issues, and lifestyle factors weaken bones silently, leading to a high risk of permanent disability from falls.

The Warning Signs

Osteoporosis is often called the "Silent Thief" because you can't feel bones thinning. Look out for these physical clues:

Loss of Height

Noticing you are 2cm or more shorter than you used to be.

Stooped Posture

A curve in the upper back or a change in how your clothes fit.

Weakened Grip

Finding it harder to open jars or lift heavy bags.

The "Easy" Break

A fracture caused by a minor trip or a strong sneeze.
