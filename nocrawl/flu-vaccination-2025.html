Why You Should Get a Flu Vaccination Every Year

1. Why Is a Yearly Flu Shot Necessary?

Influenza (flu) viruses change constantly. Each year, new strains circulate — which means last year’s vaccine may not protect you this year. In addition, your immunity from previous flu shots naturally declines over time. An annual flu vaccine is your best defence against the latest flu viruses.

2. Protect Yourself and Those Around You

Getting vaccinated helps reduce your risk of falling seriously ill from the flu. But it’s not just about you — it also protects vulnerable individuals in your community, including young children, seniors, and people with chronic conditions.

3. Is the Flu Vaccine Safe?

Yes. The flu vaccine is updated and tested yearly for safety and effectiveness. Most people experience mild side effects like soreness at the injection site. Serious side effects are extremely rare — and the benefits far outweigh the risks.

4. Who Should Get the Flu Vaccine?

The Ministry of Health Singapore recommends the flu vaccine for everyone aged 6 months and above — especially:

  • Seniors aged 65 and above
  • Pregnant women
  • Young children
  • People with chronic illnesses (e.g., diabetes, asthma, heart disease)
  • Healthcare workers and caregivers

5. When Should You Get Vaccinated?

In Singapore, flu can occur year-round — but the ideal time to get vaccinated is between September and November, before peak flu season. The earlier you get protected, the better.

6. What Is Influenza?

Influenza is a contagious viral infection that affects the respiratory system. It can cause fever, chills, cough, sore throat, muscle aches, and fatigue. In severe cases, it may lead to complications like pneumonia or hospitalisation.
