Long COVID Support

When recovery doesn't feel complete, we help you look deeper.

If you are experiencing lingering symptoms months after your initial COVID-19 infection, you may be dealing with Long COVID (also known as post-COVID condition). These symptoms can vary widely and may affect your physical, mental, or cognitive function.

we provide comprehensive support through a multidisciplinary approach. Our doctors assess your symptoms holistically and may involve specialists and allied health professionals to address the diverse range of issues you may be facing.

We also collaborate with rehabilitation teams, including physiotherapy and occupational therapy, to support your recovery of strength, stamina, and daily functioning. For individuals experiencing cognitive symptoms, anxiety, or mood changes, we provide structured support and appropriate care pathways.

Common Long Covid symptoms include: persistent fatigue or brain fog, chest discomfort or shortness of breath, palpitations or irregular heartbeat, sleep disturbances or headaches, low mood, anxiety, or irritability, muscle aches, joint pain, or lingering pain.

Let us help you evaluate ongoing symptoms with targeted physical examinations and investigations to rule out other underlying causes.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What happens when you are tested positive?

    After seeing doctor and prescribed medication, you are encouraged to stay at home, take your medication, and recover with minimal contact with outsiders. Please continue to self-test with ART kit during this recovery period.

    You are not encouraged to travel or go to crowded places when you are tested COVID-19 positive. Since this is an infectious virus, please minimise COVID-19 spread to your family members, loved ones and others.

  • What is Long COVID?

    Long COVID refers to persistent symptoms lasting weeks or months after the initial COVID-19 infection. Symptoms include fatigue, shortness of breath, brain fog, and joint pain. It can affect anyone, regardless of initial severity. Comprehensive care is crucial for managing these prolonged health issues effectively.

  • How do you classify Long COVID?

    Long COVID is defined by the World Health Organization (WHO) and other health authorities as symptoms lasting more than four weeks after the initial COVID-19 infection. These symptoms can persist for weeks, months, or even longer, affecting various aspects of health and well-being.

  • Why do I need to get treated for Long COVID?

    Treating Long COVID is essential because it helps alleviate persistent symptoms that can significantly impact a person's quality of life and daily functioning. Effective treatment aims to manage symptoms such as fatigue, respiratory issues, cognitive difficulties, and others that may persist for weeks or months after the initial infection. Additionally, early intervention and comprehensive care can potentially prevent long-term health complications associated with prolonged COVID-19 symptoms.
