Deciphering the Signal: Is it Your Heart or Your Mind?

When your chest tightens or your pulse accelerates, your brain immediately panics. However, different cardiovascular and psychological imbalances trigger entirely unique patterns under the surface:

Clinical Feature Intermittent Palpitations 💓 High Heart Rate (Tachycardia) ⏱️ Anxiety-Induced Racing 🧠
What It Feels Like A sudden skip, hard thud, sudden drop, or erratic "fluttering" in the chest or throat. A sustained, rhythmic, unprovoked pounding. It feels like you are sprinting while sitting still. A rapid heartbeat accompanied by a sense of dread, cold sweats, and tightening chest muscles.
The Common Trigger Often random; can happen while resting, watching television, or lying flat in bed at night. Physical exertion, chronic dehydration, systemic fever, or underlying tissue irritation. Acute emotional stressors, hidden cognitive worries, or a sudden surge of fight-or-flight adrenaline.
The Rhythm Pattern Irregular & erratic. The baseline heart rhythm breaks unexpectedly for a few fleeting beats. Regular but rapid. The beat remains uniform and steady, but highly accelerated (>100 BPM). Regular and smooth. It accelerates gradually as worry mounts and tapers slowly as you calm down.

Note: These states do not live in isolated boxes. A single benign palpitation can ignite panic, which then drives your heart rate even higher. Trying to untangle this feedback loop from a one-off laboratory report is why patients stay stuck in cycles of worry.
