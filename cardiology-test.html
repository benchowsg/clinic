Cardiovascular Health Insights

Common Heart Problems

The five most common conditions we manage are Coronary Artery Disease, Hypertensive Heart Disease, Heart Failure, Arrhythmias, and Valvular Heart Disease. Often, these conditions are linked by shared risks like diabetes and high cholesterol.

Can a Weak Heart Recover?

In many cases, yes. Modern pharmacology (ARNIs, SGLT2i) helps the heart remodel. Combined with cardiac rehab and treating root causes like sleep apnea or alcohol use, function can improve dramatically.

Heart-Healthy Hydration

Plain water is the gold standard. Unsweetened green tea and moderate coffee are generally safe, but sugary soft drinks and energy drinks should be avoided to protect weight and blood pressure.

Cardiology FAQ

Early signs are often subtle: shortness of breath when climbing stairs or lying flat, unusual fatigue, and swelling in the ankles or feet. Needing more pillows to sleep or waking up breathless at night are particularly concerning red flags that require medical attention.
