Why an Asian Context Matters

International calculators often underestimate risk for Asian populations. In Singapore, South Asians (Indians) have statistically higher rates of coronary artery disease, while the Malay community often presents with earlier metabolic shifts. Furthermore, Asians have higher visceral fat at lower BMIs (the "Thin-on-the-Outside, Fat-on-the-Inside" phenotype), meaning heart risk can be high even if you appear lean.
